## Epic Summary

Epic 5 delivered the contact and visitor intelligence experience:

- Contact form rebuilt with full 6-state UX model (Empty, Filling, Validating, Submitting, Success, Error), accessible validation (visible labels, `aria-describedby`, `aria-live`), blur-driven validation, reCAPTCHA hardening with expiry/error handling, timeout-bounded EmailJS submission, and FR22 contact information panel sourced from `src/data/`.
- Visitor tracking expanded from 9 to 20+ data fields: browser fingerprint (name/version/OS/device type), screen resolution, viewport size, UTM parameters (5 fields), language, connection type, ISP. Geolocation failure no longer aborts the entire flow — fallback values are used while browser/device/referral data still proceeds.
- Timeout handling added to both ipapi.co fetch (8s) and EmailJS send (10s) to prevent indefinite pending states.
- Two different AI agents executed successfully: GPT-5 Codex (Story 5.1) and Claude Opus 4.6 (Story 5.2).

Both stories are marked `done` in `sprint-status.yaml`. Across Stories 5.1–5.2, 18 review findings were recorded; 8 were patched, 7 deferred, 3 dismissed. Epic 5 added 7 new deferred items.

## What Went Well

- **100% delivery, zero rollbacks, zero reopens.** Second consecutive epic with a clean delivery record.
- **Visitor intelligence significantly expanded.** Data fields grew from 9 to 20+, with UTM capture, browser fingerprint, device type, and graceful degradation when geolocation fails. Real business value for understanding traffic sources.
- **Contact form accessibility foundation is strong.** Full 6-state UX model, visible labels, `aria-describedby`, `aria-live`, blur-driven validation, timeout-bounded submission. Solid base for Epic 6 accessibility stories.
- **Story preparation quality enabled multi-agent execution.** Both stories had comprehensive dev notes that caught stale planning docs, identified exact code locations, and warned about gotchas. Both GPT-5 Codex and Claude Opus executed cleanly from these specs.
- **Review quality caught real bugs.** Double-click duplicate email sends (Story 5.1), captcha expiry overwriting submission state (Story 5.1), ungated diagnostic console.log (Story 5.2), missing UTM template fields (Story 5.2). All genuine issues, not nitpicks.
- **Graceful geolocation failure is a real architectural win.** Refactoring from "return null and abort everything" to "always return data with fallbacks" means visitor tracking is resilient, not fragile.
- **Epic 4 forward-planning items successfully carried.** Card consolidation, sizing constraints, and preview layout requirements all incorporated into Epic 6 Story 6.1 acceptance criteria.

## Challenges

- **`project-context.md` stale for 4 consecutive epics.** Every story since Epic 1 has explicitly warned agents to ignore the styled-components guidance. Both Epic 5 stories carried the same warning. This is now chronic process waste, not a one-time oversight.
- **Retrospectives produce commitments but not accountability.** Epic 4 retro committed to 12 action items. Only 4 were fully closed. The pattern is consistent across multiple epics: forward delivery is excellent, maintenance commitments are consistently dropped.
- **Deferred work pile grows but never shrinks.** Epic 5 added 7 new items. The pile from Epics 1–4 was never meaningfully reduced. The Epic 4 retro explicitly committed to giving every deferred item a disposition — that commitment was not fully honored.
- **Manual verification structurally limited.** Both stories deferred interactive testing (EmailJS sends, reCAPTCHA flows, debug output) to Warrick. AI agents cannot complete these verifications autonomously. This is a known structural limitation but creates risk when stories advance to `done` without full agent-side verification.
- **`Promise.race` timer leak deferred twice from the same root cause.** Story 5.1 deferred it, Story 5.2 deferred it again. The pattern is noticed in every review but never resolved.
- **Documentation drift is chronic.** Card numbering in planning docs still says Contact is card 5 (live code: card 6). Planning language still references "Cards 2-5" as interactive. Every story prep has to compensate for these inaccuracies.
- **No maintenance window between epics.** Maintenance items (doc updates, deferred triage, skill badges) have no sprint to live in. Epics start immediately after the previous one closes, squeezing out non-delivery work.

## Key Decisions Made

- **Warrick will run a course correction before Epic 6.** The course correction will triage all ~30 open deferred items and assign each to an existing Epic 6 story or a new dedicated story. No item will enter Epic 6 without a disposition. This addresses the accountability gap that retrospectives alone have not solved.
- **`project-context.md` update must happen before Epic 6 story creation.** Fourth consecutive epic flagging this. The course correction is the vehicle to finally execute it.
- **Epic 6 remains the final epic.** Single production release after all stories are complete and tested to Warrick's satisfaction.
