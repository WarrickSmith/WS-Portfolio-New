## Challenges

### Deferred Item Accumulation (Primary Issue)

Deferred items accumulated across Stories 6.2 through 6.6 despite:
- Epic 4 and Epic 5 retrospectives both flagging the growing deferred pile
- Warrick explicitly mandating no-deferral in story requirements
- The pattern repeating across three consecutive epics

Story 6.7 (catch-all) successfully closed the backlog, but its necessity represented a process failure. Issues should have been fixed at the story where they were discovered rather than deferred and batched.

### Retrospective Action Items Ineffective for Behavior Change

This project demonstrated that retrospective action items are not an effective mechanism for changing agent behavior during story execution. Process-level mandates ("don't defer issues") exist in writing but lack enforcement at the point of execution. Across Epic 4, 5, and 6, the same behavioral pattern repeated despite documented commitments to change it.

### Agent Model Mid-Epic Switch

Stories 6.1-6.3 used Claude Opus 4.6; Stories 6.4-6.8 used GPT-5 Codex. The Codex stories showed a more conservative review approach with higher defer rates (Story 6.4 alone deferred 9 items).

### Mobile Performance Gap

FCP (1.73s) and LCP (2.74s) missed PRD targets of <1.5s and <2.5s respectively. Documented and accepted rather than pursued with scope-expanding changes.
