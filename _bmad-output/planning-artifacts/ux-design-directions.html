Glass Depth

Layered glass surfaces with lift-on-hover and top-edge light reflection

Cards feel like frosted glass panels floating above the dark canvas. Hover lifts cards vertically with a subtle shadow bloom and reveals a top-edge highlight that simulates overhead light catching the surface. The effect is architectural — like gallery panels suspended in a dark exhibition space.

DIRECTION B

Warm Glow

Gold-accented radiance with gentle scale and ambient light bleed

Cards respond to hover with a warm gold gradient bleeding from the corner, paired with a subtle scale-up (1.02x). The effect feels like each card is gently illuminated from within. More expressive than Glass Depth — the gold accent is a visible presence on hover rather than a subtle hint. Rounded corners (16px) soften the overall feel.

DIRECTION C

Minimal Edge

Sharp geometry, transparent backgrounds, border-only hover with gold accent line

Cards are defined purely by their borders — no background fill in resting state. The dark page shows through, creating a blueprint/wireframe aesthetic. Hover replaces the subtle border with a crisp gold accent line. Tight 4px border-radius gives a technical, precise feel. This is the most restrained direction — maximum confidence, minimum embellishment.

DIRECTION DRECOMMENDED

Layered Surface

Gradient card surfaces with cursor-tracking radial glow and subtle lift

Cards have a gentle diagonal gradient (#171717 → #131313) creating depth even at rest. On hover, a radial gold glow follows the cursor position across the card surface, combined with a minimal 1px lift and deepened shadow. The cursor-tracking glow creates an interactive, tactile quality — the card responds to where you are on it, not just whether you're on it. This is the most engaging hover effect while remaining firmly premium.

Design Direction Comparison

Side-by-side analysis of all four directions

Card Hover Treatment

A: Glass Depth

Vertical lift (translateY -2px) + shadow bloom + top-edge light highlight

+ Architectural, sophisticated, gallery-like

− Most subtle — risk of feeling flat on casual hover

B: Warm Glow

Scale up (1.02x) + gold gradient bleed from corner + ambient shadow

+ Warm, inviting, gold accent is visible and branded

− Scale effect can feel "bouncy" if not tuned carefully

C: Minimal Edge

Border colour change to gold + transparent background tint

+ Maximum restraint, technical precision, confident

− May feel too cold for "impressive" emotional goal

D: Layered Surface

Cursor-tracking radial gold glow + subtle lift + deepened shadow

+ Most interactive, tactile, rewards exploration

− Cursor tracking requires JS; slightly more complex

Border Radius

A: Glass Depth — 12px

Modern, softened. Industry standard for premium cards.

B: Warm Glow — 16px

Warmer, friendlier. Softer presence on the page.

C: Minimal Edge — 4px

Sharp, technical. Blueprint / engineering aesthetic.

D: Layered Surface — 12px

Modern, balanced. Professional without being cold.

Best Fit for Project Goals

Recommendation: Direction D (Layered Surface) as primary, with elements from A (Glass Depth)

Why D: The cursor-tracking glow creates the "wow" factor from the first hover. It's interactive and tactile — visitors feel the card responding to them, not just changing state. This creates the "I want to click the next one" response identified in the experience criteria. The gradient surface adds depth without weight.

Borrow from A: The top-edge light highlight from Glass Depth adds a refined finishing touch. Apply it to the expanded card overlay to differentiate the expansion layer from the grid layer.

Why not B: Scale effects risk feeling playful rather than premium. The gold corner gradient is beautiful but may fight with the cursor-tracking glow for attention.

Why not C: Too restrained for the "impressed" emotional goal. The transparent backgrounds undermine the premium depth layering established in the visual foundation. Better suited to a developer tools landing page than a portfolio meant to impress.
